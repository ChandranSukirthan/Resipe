Ribollita


Ingredients

  • Cannellini Beans: 1 cup dried or 2 cans (rinsed and drained)
  • Kale or Cavolo Nero: 1 bunch, chopped
  • Carrots: 2, diced
  • Onions: 2, finely chopped
  • Tomatoes: 2 large, chopped (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)
  • Potatoes: 2 medium, peeled and diced
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Cabbage: 1/2 small head, shredded
  • Swiss Chard: 1 bunch, chopped
  • Day-Old Bread: 1 loaf, preferably a rustic Italian bread, sliced
  • Vegetable Broth: 4-6 cups, depending on desired thickness
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil: 1/4 cup, plus extra for serving
  • Salt and Pepper: to taste
  • Fresh Rosemary and Thyme: a few sprigs, tied together with kitchen twine


Instructions

  • If using dried beans, soak them overnight, then cook until tender. If using canned beans, rinse and drain.
  • In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, carrots, and celery, and sauté until softened.
  • Add garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  • Add potatoes, tomatoes, cabbage, kale, and Swiss chard to the pot. Stir well.
  • Pour in the vegetable broth, add the tied herbs, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  • Add the cooked or canned beans to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Break the slices of day-old bread into pieces and stir them into the soup. The bread will dissolve and thicken the soup.
  • Let the soup simmer for about 20-30 minutes until all the flavors meld together
  • Remove the tied herbs from the soup before serving.
  • Serve the Ribollita hot, drizzled with extra virgin olive oil and accompanied by additional crusty bread if desired.
